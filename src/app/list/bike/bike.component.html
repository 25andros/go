Details about app

This app is designed to show how far each gear whilst on a bicycle will take you.

How to use

On the left side of the screen, is where the user will select the different parts on their bicycle. For instance, if the user has a road bicycle, they would select the chainring, the cassette on their bike, their tire size and wheel type. In a road bikes case, they would selected a road chainring, the appropriate cassette, a 700c wheel and most likely a 25mm wide tire.

Making sense of the numbers

The meters of development table show the amount of speed that will occur in one revolution of the cranks, aka the pedals.

Definitions:

chainring: The front sprocket/gears located at the bottom of the frame

cassette: The rear sprocket/gears located on the rear wheel
